Welcome

Welcome to my Linguistics research portfolio and project page, where I nerd out about linguistics!

Where It All Started

My first exposure to formal Linguistics came from attending Ohio State's Summer Linguistics for Youth Institute (SLIYS, pronounced "slice") in the summer before my sophomore year. Before then, I'd always been passionate about language, but I had no idea that language could be studied as a science.

Discovering Computational Linguistics

At SLIYS, I discovered the field of computational linguistics. "If a machine has to do X in order to perform this linguistic task, do humans have to do X as well?" This really fascinated me—human language informs machines, but machines can also inform our understanding of human language.

Language and Meaning

Meanwhile, I was also becoming more interested in translation. As I learned Chinese and French more deeply, I started experimenting with expressing the same idea in different languages. I especially loved the nuance—like how "conscience" in French means both "consciousness" and "conscience."

NACLO & Programming

In my sophomore year, I began taking NACLO seriously. I studied, practiced, and eventually qualified for the Invitational Round!

I also got into competitive programming, especially problems involving strings or language. But when chatting with my friends in China, I realized that programming—in English—was often an extra challenge for them.

Bridging Gaps

This was a struggle my dad faced as a clinical analyst. So I thought: how could I help? Tutorials in native languages? A Python preprocessor for Chinese coders? I eventually settled on translating competitive programming problems into Chinese.

NLP & the Human Brain

That summer, I took a class on Artificial Intelligence, Natural Language Processing, and Semantics with Prof. Mohammed Taher Pilehvar from Cambridge.
